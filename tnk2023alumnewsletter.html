Texnikoi Newsletter Autumn 2023

Hello everyone! To reconnect and keep TNK alumni updated with the organization, we are excited to announce our semesterly newsletter. These emails will typically contain updates about the organization, students, alumni, and the College of Engineering, as well as excerpts from our archives. We will start off with a CoE update from the Associate Dean Dr. David Tomasko and Dean Dr. Annya Howard!

Hey TNKer's! 

Dean Howard and Associate Dean Tomasko here. We wanted to say hello and give big shout out to Texnikoi for being one of our longest running and most productive student organizations. Ben, your new president, asked us to provide a short update on the College for this newsletter (and we thought - what a great idea!) so here it goes. Drum roll please… 

 

Number one - Ohio State continues to be the absolute best engineering college in the state and one of the strongest in the country. Coming in at #27 in the country in the 2024 USNWR rankings, this was a 3-point jump shot from #30 (and matches our highest ranking in the past decade). You, of course, are one of the main reasons that others see us as a quality institution so keep representing! 

 

Number two - We have also added a new degree program in Engineering Technology. It's a 4 year BS degree offered primarily at the regional campuses with an emphasis on manufacturing to support the resurgence of industry in Ohio. We will have the first class of graduates in Spring of 2024. You have also undoubtedly heard about Intel moving to town along with much of their supply chain so we are seeing renewed interest in microelectronics and high tech manufacturing. New certificates and minors in semiconductor devices and processing were just approved this past year. So, check it out and get certified during your time here at Ohio State! 

 

Number three - If you haven't been back to campus recently, it's almost unrecognizable with new energy and interdisciplinary research buildings going up on west campus and the second phase of the Biomedical and Materials Engineering Complex (yes, that’s a mouthful). Situated at the corner of Woodruff and College, this will be the new home to the first-year engineering program so we have dubbed it the "Gateway to Engineering". And fasten your seatbelts – new makerspaces will be a key asset in the new space. We can’t wait to see what amazing inventions get created! 

 

All in all it's an exciting time in the College. We invite you to stop by Hitchcock if you're on campus to say Hi and let us know what you're doing. Go Bucks! Best regards. Dr. H and Dr. T


What are TNKers up to?


Former TNK VP Sophia Sperry just graduated with a degree in Chemical Engineering with a minor in Humanitarian Engineering. She recently began working as a project engineer at P&G's Cascade facility in St Louis. Sophia says, "TNK gave me a community within engineering outside of my specific discipline. I'm thankful for the network of tnkers across the US I keep in touch with. It also prepared me for all the pizza recs I'd get at work"

 

Mark DiFranco, Mechanical Engineering '24, interned at ABB as a proposals engineer where he worked on three quarter million dollar technology upgrade projects. Right after finishing at ABB, Mark worked at a pizza place. He says, "I felt like at the beginning I needed my hand held at ABB, but now I feel like I'm very independent and get my work done efficiently"

Fred Ehrman retired from ExxonMobil in 2021 as Chief Engineer Polymers Process Technology. He worked with R&D Teams on all phases of commercialization from catalyst discovery to pilot testing to commercial trails. He says, "Mentoring young engineers was a particular joy, with this type of activity continuing after retirement through SCORE small business consulting. The OSU ChE program proved to be an excellent practical and theoretical foundation for success."

Since 2019, Lorie Terry (ChemE ‘99) has been a plant manager at Crown Holdings, one of the largest metal packaging manufacturers. She works to keep the plant running smoothly and to increase efficiency. Lorie began at Crown after working in both the paper and activated carbon industries.

Dr. Patrick Sours joins Kent Halloran as Co-Advisor

Exciting news! We welcome Dr. Patrick Sours, a TNK alumnus (BS ‘17 MS ‘19 Ph.D ‘23), as our new Faculty Co-advisor. He's a FABE Senior Lecturer at The Ohio State University, specializing in Engineering for Sustainable Development and Humanitarian Engineering. With a focus on sociotechnical competency, he aims to tackle challenges in diverse cultural contexts. Dr. Sours has earned recognition for exceptional teaching and leads sustainability projects, including safe water delivery to rural communities. He joins Kent Halloran (BS ‘84), long-time advisor and TNK alumnus. We are excited to utilize their extensive knowledge bases for our upcoming 100th anniversary.

Looking Ahead

Our plans for Autumn 2023 are quite exciting. At the retreat this past May, we voted to raise the incoming TNK membership to 30 from the previous 25. With serveral legacy applicants we expect to have an excellent group of students applying. Several other things we are hoping to accomplish include:  
  • A joint meeting with Engineers Without Borders (EWB) to create a relationship between both organizations and to allow members of both groups to socialize and build their networks.      
  • Professional development events, including alumni-student advice Q&As and resume reviews. (Thanks for filling out those surveys!)
  • An Olentangy River cleanup - a service event to beautify the ever important trail into campus.
  • Increase TNK's prescence as an mainstay engineering organization at OSU through involvement and service.

Life’s Journey

By Kent Halloran (BSCE ’85)

Hello my fellow TNKer alumni. To quote from the Rolling Stones: “Please allow me to introduce myself.” I am not only an alum of Texnikoi, but I also have the honor as serving as their advisor. And before you ask, I understand the phrase I quoted is from the song “Sympathy for the Devil.” It wasn’t a wholly inappropriate selection.

It seems like only yesterday… No, it doesn’t… Hmmm, a short time ago… nope… not THAT long ago, when I collected my degree at The Shoe on a hot June day. Yet, as you can tell from my by-line, more than a few decades have passed. Back then it never occurred to me that I would one day return to Ohio State as an employee, let alone advise my old honorary. Yet here I am…

All TNKer alumni have a story to tell. Many of you have just started your professional life, others of us have a longer journey to share. How our decisions impacted our journey and those of our loved ones. Perhaps now is the time, the time to meet new friends and rekindle old acquaintances. I am proud to be the first to contribute to our new Texnikoi alumni newsletter.

Please rejoin us and tell us your story. Share with all your Texnikoi clan, alumni and current members alike, some of the lessons you have learnt along your journey, or perhaps a few thoughts or philosophical musings. If you are a member of LinkedIn, please visit our Texnikoi Group.

Our new officers and members are eager to establish an active Alumni network. Please join us.

To connect with other TNK alumni, post resumes, job openings, or post anything else TNK related is our LinkedIn page! Current students will be posting resumes looking for suggestions this upcoming semester.
